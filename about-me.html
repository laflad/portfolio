A little about me!

How I got to where I am today.

I'm originally from Québec, Canada in a little town far north called Val d'Or where no one spoke English. So, French is my first language. Some would say I have no accent, but that's because I was immersed in American culture when I was twelve years old.

In 1997, My family moved from Québec to Tempe, Arizona in the middle of winter. I got off the plane in shorts and a t-shirt while the locals around were fully covered head to toe. It took me a few years to get acclimated, but I got used to it.

Fast forward to today and I'm now back to a climate close to my original home. I love Washington state and all that it has to offer. When I'm not working, I love to play in my garden or discover new hikes. The winters are perfect for me to hibernate and relax inside while enjoying the rain.

My work experience journey has been an interesting one and I wouldn't change it for anything. Out of high school, I attended community college for accounting. Most of my family members have careers revolving around numbers so it just made sense. I quickly learned that I was not built for accounting. The short story is I went from accounting to operations, to marketing and photography, to now design. After starting Marketing, I quickly realized my potential and decided to back to a full 4-year college to obtain a degree. So in my 30's I finally obtained my B.S. in Business Marketing from Menlo College. I now leverage my design experience with my degree and it partners really well for my work.

Now, I'm embarking on Front-End Development to partner with my design experience. I'm excited to see what comes of it!

- David
